# Interview-Ready Case Study Pack

## Case Study 1: Developer Onboarding Acceleration

### Situation
Application teams faced fragmented onboarding workflows across Kubernetes, CI/CD, networking, observability, and operations readiness.

### Task
Improve onboarding speed and reduce developer dependency on manual platform support.

### Action
- Synthesized pain points from support tickets, stakeholder feedback, and workflow analysis
- Defined a golden-path onboarding experience
- Prioritized self-service workflows and readiness checklists
- Partnered with engineering and platform teams to standardize requirements
- Introduced telemetry to measure adoption, cycle time, and blockers

### Result
Representative outcomes included faster onboarding, higher platform adoption, reduced support dependency, and better roadmap decisions through product health metrics.

### Interview Framing
This example demonstrates technical product leadership, developer empathy, cross-functional execution, and outcome-based prioritization.

## Case Study 2: Self-Service LBaaS

### Situation
Routine load-balancing requests created repeated manual work for platform and network teams.

### Task
Create a scalable self-service model while maintaining governance for production traffic changes.

### Action
- Defined standard request patterns
- Scoped a guided intake workflow
- Added validation and approval gates
- Designed metrics for cycle time, adoption, failed requests, and support reduction
- Balanced speed with operational and security risk

### Result
The product direction reduced BAU dependency, improved request quality, and created a measurable path for platform modernization.

### Interview Framing
This example demonstrates platform PM judgment: automate what is repeatable, govern what is risky, and measure business impact.

## Case Study 3: AI-Assisted Platform Support

### Situation
Developers frequently needed help navigating documentation, onboarding steps, and troubleshooting.

### Task
Improve support scalability and developer experience without replacing expert review for high-risk changes.

### Action
- Identified repeat support questions and documentation gaps
- Embedded AI-assisted support into the workflow
- Added feedback loops for answer quality
- Defined guardrails for escalation and unsupported cases
- Measured support deflection and satisfaction

### Result
The approach improved developer self-service and reduced repetitive support demand while keeping human review for risk-sensitive decisions.

### Interview Framing
This example demonstrates practical AI product thinking: workflow integration, guardrails, feedback, and measurable outcomes.

## Case Study 4: Cloud Migration Delivery Leadership

### Situation
Application migration across cloud environments required coordination across app teams, platform, networking, security, data, and operations.

### Task
Drive cross-functional execution, clarify ownership, and move teams through environment gates.

### Action
- Created onboarding sequence across prerequisites, tenant/platform setup, app deployment, validation, and handoff
- Clarified roles across delivery lead, embedded engineer, app team, platform, and operations
- Managed dependencies such as DNS, gateways, load balancing, storage, database, and observability
- Used structured status updates to surface blockers and decisions

### Result
The work reduced ambiguity, improved execution visibility, and helped teams progress through migration milestones.

### Interview Framing
This example demonstrates senior stakeholder management, technical fluency, and program/product operating discipline.
