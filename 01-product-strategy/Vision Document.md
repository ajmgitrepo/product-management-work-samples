**Vision Document: Stand-Alone Enterprise-Grade Swarm Product**

**Vision Statement:** "Our vision is to create a stand-alone enterprise-grade Swarm product that caters to a distinct group of users and customers seeking an alternative to Kubernetes. This product will address the needs of individuals and organizations that appreciate the simplicity and effectiveness of Swarm, while also alleviating their concerns about its long-term viability. We aim to empower our existing Swarm users, who have expressed genuine enthusiasm for its usage, by delivering on their feature requests and ensuring a stable, dependable future for the Swarm platform."

**Problem Statement:** The current container orchestration landscape presents challenges for users and organizations seeking alternatives to Kubernetes. While Docker Swarm has gained a dedicated user base that values its simplicity and effectiveness, concerns about its long-term viability have persisted, creating uncertainty among existing/prospective users. Additionally, large enterprises with global operations and security-focused sectors of the federal government are hesitant to adopt Kubernetes due to their unique requirements and concerns about security. These groups lack a solution that offers both the familiarity of Swarm and the enterprise-grade features required for managing large deployments and mission-critical applications.

**Solution:** Develop a stand-alone enterprise-grade Swarm product that addresses the needs and concerns of the identified targeted users. This product will serve users and organizations seeking an alternative to Kubernetes while delivering a seamless experience and alleviating concerns about Swarm's future. The solution comprises several key components:

1. **User-Centric Innovation:** Our core objective is to develop a Swarm product that directly addresses the needs and preferences of embedded Swarm users. We will prioritize features that enhance user experience, streamline deployment management, and ensure ease of use. The UX design will ensure a seamless experience for both new and experienced users, great care will be taken to avoid adding unnecessary product complexity.

2. **Long-Term Viability:** We are committed to dispelling uncertainties about Swarm's future by demonstrating our dedication to its continued development and support. Our product will be built with a clear roadmap for updates, improvements, and long-term sustainability.

3. **Feature Fulfillment:** We will actively engage with existing Swarm users to understand their feature requests and pain points. Our goal is to deliver on these requests, enhancing the capabilities of Swarm and ensuring that it remains a valuable solution for their container orchestration needs.

4. **Enterprise-Grade Reliability:** Recognizing the importance of Swarm to some large enterprises with global operations, we will focus on providing enterprise-grade reliability, scalability, and performance. Our product will be designed to handle the demands of mission-critical applications.

5. **Government Sector Penetration:** In sectors of the federal government where security is paramount, we aim to position our enhanced Swarm product as an attractive and secure alternative to Kubernetes. By addressing security concerns and incorporating features for managing extensive deployments, we intend to gain a foothold in this critical market segment.

**Collaboration and Feedback:** We recognize that achieving our vision requires an ongoing dialogue with our users, stakeholders, and/or any other group that may have a touchpoint in product development/delivery. We are committed to actively listening to feedback, iterating based on user input, and fostering a collaborative environment. By engaging in a two-way conversation, we will ensure that our product aligns with real-world needs and remains a viable container orchestration solution.

**Known Risks/Issues:** 

1. **Limited Ecosystem Integration:** Swarm's integrations are limited compared to Kubernetes 

2. **Sparse Community Support:** The open-source community around Swarm is smaller compared to Kubernetes, resulting in fewer resources, tutorials, and community-driven solutions available to users.

3. **Scaling Challenges:** While Swarm offers scalability, it may face limitations in handling extremely large deployments compared to Kubernetes.

**Enhancement Candidates:**  Based on current knowledge, Swarm has some distinct disadvantages that prevent it from being a viable solution for larger deployments. We will work with engineering to fully vet all known issues, determine the technical feasibility of modifying the current architecture, and identify/prioritize enhancement opportunities. However, here’s a list of possible areas to be targeted:

1. **Enhanced Cluster Management:** Introduce advanced cluster management features such as automated scaling, load balancing, and failover to provide users with greater control over their deployments.

2. **Improved Ecosystem Integrations:** Develop plugins and integrations that expand Swarm's compatibility with popular DevOps tools, monitoring systems, and CI/CD pipelines.

3. **Advanced Security Features:** Address the concerns of security-focused sectors by adding features like enhanced access controls, compliance auditing, and encryption mechanisms.

4. **Auto-Healing Mechanisms:** Implement automated recovery mechanisms that detect and rectify faults within the Swarm clusters, improving reliability and minimizing downtime.

5. **Resource Management:** Develop tools for efficient resource allocation and optimization, allowing users to maximize the utilization of hardware resources

**Conclusion:**

In conclusion, our goal is to create a stand-alone enterprise-grade Swarm product that caters to users seeking an alternative to Kubernetes. We aim to empower existing Swarm users, address concerns about viability, and enhance the platform's features. Collaboration and feedback will drive our approach, ensuring user-centric innovation. While acknowledging known risks and limitations, we'll collaborate with engineering to explore enhancements in areas like cluster management, integrations, security, auto-healing, and resource management. Ultimately, we intend to establish Swarm as a preferred solution that excels in simplicity, reliability, and security for container orchestration needs.
